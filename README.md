# Economic Development and Acid‑Rain‑Related Emissions  
**A Cross‑Country Exploratory Analysis**

## 📌 Project Overview
This project explores the relationship between **economic development** and emissions of **sulphur dioxide (SO₂)** and **nitrogen oxides (NOₓ)** — two key pollutants associated with acid rain.

Using cross‑country data, the analysis examines how emissions patterns differ across countries at different income levels, with a focus on **emissions intensity** rather than absolute emissions. The project is exploratory and descriptive, aiming to identify broad patterns and outliers rather than establish causality.

This work forms part of my analytics portfolio and reflects a long‑standing interest in **environmental policy, sustainable development, and international cooperation on air‑quality issues**.

---

## 🎯 Research Question
> **Is there a relationship between a country’s level of economic development and its reliance on fuels and energy systems that emit SO₂ and NOₓ?**

Specifically, the analysis explores whether **lower‑income countries exhibit higher emissions intensity** (per capita and per unit of GDP) compared with higher‑income countries.

---

## 🧭 Analytical Approach
- Cross‑country exploratory data analysis
- Normalisation of emissions:
  - Per capita
  - Per unit of GDP
- Grouping and comparison by income level
- Identification and discussion of outliers

The project deliberately avoids causal claims and instead focuses on **descriptive patterns** that are relevant for policy discussion and international comparisons.

---

## 📊 Data Sources
The analysis uses publicly available international datasets, including:

- **Economic indicators** (GDP, GDP per capita)
- **Country‑level emissions inventories** for:
  - Sulphur dioxide (SO₂)
  - Nitrogen oxides (NOₓ)

All data sources are internationally recognised and suitable for cross‑country comparison, though data quality and reporting standards vary by country.

---

## 📐 Key Metrics
- GDP
- GDP per capita
- SO₂ emissions
- NOₓ emissions
- SO₂ emissions per unit of GDP
- NOₓ emissions per unit of GDP
- Per‑capita emissions measures

---

## 🔍 Expected Areas of Insight
The analysis explores:
- Whether emissions intensity declines as income rises
- Differences in emissions profiles between income groups
- The extent to which GDP alone explains observed emissions patterns
- Notable outliers that may reflect regulation, energy mix, or resource endowment

---

## ⚠️ Limitations
- The analysis does **not** infer causality
- Emissions are influenced by regulation, technology, energy resources, and industrial structure, not income alone
- Data quality and reporting consistency vary across countries
- Results should be interpreted as **indicative patterns**, not definitive conclusions

Explicitly stating these limitations is an intentional part of the analytical design.
